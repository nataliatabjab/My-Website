Development of a Cryogenic Tunnel Diode Oscillator for Superconducting Penetration-Depth Measurements

Natalia Tabja • August 2025

Motivation

Measurements of the London penetration depth λL and contact-free resistivity provide key insights into the order parameter and pairing mechanisms of unconventional superconductors. Tunnel-diode oscillators (TDOs)—high-frequency LC circuits that self-resonate due to the negative differential resistance of a tunnel diode—offer unparalleled sensitivity to λL(T).

Plan

This project will adapt the current room-temperature TDO circuit into a stable low-temperature probe. The design will integrate thermometry, shielding, and temperature control so that the oscillator can be stabilized at 4.2 K. Measurements will be carried out with a coil embedded in a diamond-anvil cell gasket, enabling background frequency shift determination at 4–40 K.
