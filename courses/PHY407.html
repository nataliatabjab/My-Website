PHY407 — Computational Physics

Numerical methods applied to physics: root finding, Monte-Carlo integration, finite-difference schemes for ODEs and PDEs, spectral analysis and data fitting.

View Repository ← Back to Coursework

Assignments

A1 — Iterative solvers & data fitting

Implemented the Gauss-Seidel method for linear systems and explored root-finding and model fitting on noisy data sets. Generated plots comparing convergence with and without over-relaxation and assessed the effect of precision on iteration count.

A2 — Harmonic oscillator & Monte-Carlo integration

Discretised the Schrödinger equation for the quantum harmonic oscillator, computed eigenvalues and wavefunctions, and visualised them. Evaluated multi‑dimensional integrals using importance sampling and compared relative errors across sampling strategies.

A3 — Fourier analysis & signal processing

Applied discrete Fourier transforms to noisy signals, performed convolution for smoothing and denoising, and reconstructed an audio waveform from gravitational‑wave–like data.

A4 — Partial differential equations

Developed finite‑difference schemes for the heat equation, shallow‑water equations and Burgers’ equation. Analysed numerical stability conditions and compared FTCS and leapfrog schemes.

A5 — Capstone project

Synthesised techniques from earlier assignments: solved Laplace’s equation with Gauss–Seidel relaxation (with and without over‑relaxation), computed electric field streamlines, evolved shallow‑water systems, and simulated Burgers’ equation via a leapfrog scheme.

Formal Lab 4

My final report tackles three complex systems: (1) solving Laplace’s equation on a square grid using Gauss–Seidel relaxation, comparing convergence with and without over‑relaxation; (2) simulating the one‑dimensional shallow‑water equations via an FTCS scheme and visualising wave propagation; and (3) integrating Burgers’ equation with a leapfrog method, highlighting how non‑linear steepening induces numerical oscillations.

Highlights

  • Implemented Gauss–Seidel relaxation with and without over‑relaxation to solve Laplace’s equation and computed electric field lines from the potential.
  • Developed an FTCS scheme for the shallow‑water equations and analysed how grid spacing and time step affect stability.
  • Simulated Burgers’ equation using a leapfrog method and examined how non‑linear terms lead to numerical oscillations.
  • Explored quantum harmonic oscillators, Monte‑Carlo integration and spectral analysis through earlier assignments.
